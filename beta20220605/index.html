Digital rights are basically human rights in the digital era.
The rights to online privacy, the protection of personal data, access to information and freedom of expression, are really extensions of the equal and inalienable rights laid out in the United Nation’s Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

Thus, by extension, the evolution of technology and the corresponding expansion of human activities in the digital world it is inevitable to broaden the digital rights map even further.

….Manifesto..Μανιφέστο….

The public declaration of our scope.

Considering that:

  • Recognition of the inherent dignity and of the equal and inalienable rights of all members of the human family is the foundation of freedom, justice and peace in the world, as stated in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights

  • Human in the digital age uses digital technologies for a number of reasons, including to expand own biological potential, record own thoughts, preserve own memories and consequently to improve oneself and own abilities

  • Human's relationship with digital technologies is like an organisation with its members-organs

  • The digital organs of a person can be found, as transplants, both inside (implants) and outside (explants) the biological limits of the body

  • Exclusion practices and behaviors (due to gender, race, class, ethnicity, age, sexual identity, anatomy, educational level, etc.) are represented, captured and performed in the digital landscape perpetuating inequality

We recognise that:

  • The boundaries of humans in the digital age extend beyond their biological boundaries and include all the digital instruments with which they extend

  • Digital rights are human rights in the digital age with the articles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights valid, to protect its integrity and dignity, in all the rights and freedoms proclaimed in it

Therefore,

the scope of "the binary organisation" is humanitarian, social, cultural, technological, informational and educational within the mission of the parties and fully harmonised with it. The objectives of the organisation, which is public and non-profit, consist of:

  • The creation of the right conditions for the extension of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights to the digital landscape, enhancing the right to privacy, the protection of personal data, freedom of expression, freedom of information, the right to property -material and intellectual-, the right to litigation and anti-discrimination

  • Ensuring freedom of communication, expression, privacy and pluralism in accordance with constitutional requirements and promoting the anthropocentric character of science and technology as an expression of modern culture

  • Digital rights are human rights in the digital age with the articles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights valid, to protect its integrity and dignity, in all the rights and freedoms proclaimed in it

  • Contribute to the recognition of the good of Internet access as a basic human right and its importance in achieving the UN Sustainable Development Goal, worldwide

  • Support the development and promotion of business models, by informing and guiding businesses to integrate or switch to open source, encryption, usability and interoperability solutions, without exception and with respect to digital rights

  • Covering the lack of valid, timely and centralised information, informing and sensitizing the wider community on issues of recognition and respect for digital human rights through modern media

  • Creating an environment through information and awareness-raising, where digital technologies can contribute to a fairer, more democratic and more sustainable society

Roadmap

Our five era pillars, in a infinite mindset

All the way, from the genesis block onwards we are based on a peer-reviewed social, technological and architecture research carrying an ethos of openness and transparency. All of the research and technical specifications that underpin our projects are publicly published, and all of our development activity is made available to the public.

Time is relative, binary isn’t

Our structured roadmap is a summary of our development, which has been organised into five eras: Culture, Technology, Education, Law, and Inclusion. Each era is centered around a set of deliverables that will be released across multiple project cycles.

While the eras of our scope will be delivered sequentially, the work for each era happens in parallel, with research, prototyping, and development often in progress all at once across the different research and development streams.

The work of each era is gathered and presented on its dedicated section, representing years of effort. In every area we deliver an overview of the goals of each one, as well as descriptions of the core functional components, links to associated journalistic and technological research, status updates, and even real-time commits.

Team

We are human beings, like you.

Digital technology has transformed the way we live, communicate, think, express and opened up limitless new ways to connect, share and learn across the world, but for all the benefits, technological developments have created new threats to our human rights. We stand to raise awareness of these threats and challenge them through public campaigns, media commentary, legal actions, policy interventions and tech projects.

More like a band, less like a gang.

Our core team consists of technologists, journalists, lawyers and museologists aiming in exposing the invisible digital landscape to the public, in physical world terms.
